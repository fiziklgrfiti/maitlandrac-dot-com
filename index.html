üéÅ About The Drive

What is a Reverse Advent Calendar?

We've all enjoyed an advent calendar! Receiving a little chocolate or gift each day during December is a fun way to mark the count down towards Christmas. A Reverse Advent Calendar flips this idea, encouraging participants to give an item each day for 20 days instead. At the end of the 20 days, participants have a box filled with food and essentials ready to be given to a local family in need in time for Christmas. There is no money involved (apart from purchasing the actual cardboard boxes)... just a generous exchange of food from families who can afford to give, to families who need a little help over Christmas. Neighbour to neighbour.

Where did this idea come from?

Heather Luttrell, a school teacher from Ballarat, Victoria started the drive in 2018 with the hope of filling four boxes among her family and friends. They managed to fill and donate 70 boxes! The idea quickly spread around the country, and now dozens of Australians volunteer as local coordinators. Last year, over 6000 boxes were filled across Australia, putting food in the bellies and smiles on the faces of over 6000 families who needed a little help during the festive season. You can learn more about it here: Reverse Advent Calendar ‚Äì Reverse Advent Calendar Ballarat Australia

How do you decide where the boxes go?

People who would like to start a Reverse Advent Calendar (RAC) Drive in their community volunteer to become a local coordinator. They select a local food relief agency and contact that organisation to check that they'd welcome the donation, and if they have any specific needs. There is no requirement to choose any agency or charity in particular. The Maitland RAC Drive was started by Michelle Harvey, a school teacher and mum of three from East Maitland. She chose Maitland Region Community Support as the recipients of the Maitland drive.

Why did you choose Maitland Region Community Support?

MRCS (formerly known as the Maitland Community Centre) is based in Rutherford, and has supported Maitland LGA families since 1987. They're a non-profit organisation that provides food and pharmacy relief, emergency homelessness support, bill assistance, playgroups, parenting and cooking classes. MRCS receives referrals from local schools, charities, health and community services for families who need support, and have provided these families with Christmas food hampers for a number of years. They knew who needed the help, but had not been able to meet the increasing demand for support at Christmas in our community, having more referrals than hampers. They were a natural fit and have been fantastic to work with for the last three years. You can learn more about them here: Supporting the local community | Maitland Region Community Support

How many boxes have you provided?

In 2022 the Maitland drive donated 40 boxes. In 2023, we donated 195 boxes. In 2024, we donated (at least) 450 boxes, with an additional three car loads of extra food and toiletries to Maitland Region Community Support. We also donated 10 packed shopping bags full of cereal, milk and canned fruit to Metford Public School's Breakfast Club. We're so proud of the way the Maitland community has pulled together to support this drive. That is at least 10.5 TONNES or $70000 worth of food and essentials that have made it to the homes of local Maitland families in need.

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

How many days do I need to add items to my box? ‚ñº

You add one item each day for 20 days, not the full month of December. This makes it more manageable while still creating a meaningful collection of food and essentials for a family in need.

What items should I include in my box? ‚ñº

Include non-perishable food items (canned goods, pasta, rice, breakfast cereals), toiletries (toothpaste, soap, shampoo, toilet paper), and household essentials. All items should be new and within their use-by dates. No money is involved - just the generous exchange of food and essentials from families who can afford to give to families who need help.

Do I need to provide my own box? ‚ñº

Yes, you'll need to purchase your own cardboard box - this is the only cost involved in participating. Any sturdy cardboard box that can hold 20 items will work perfectly.

Where do the boxes go once collected? ‚ñº

All boxes are donated to Maitland Region Community Support (MRCS), a non-profit organisation based in Rutherford that has supported Maitland LGA families since 1987. MRCS receives referrals from local schools, charities, and health services for families who need support, and they distribute the boxes as Christmas food hampers to local families in need.

How successful has the Maitland drive been? ‚ñº

The Maitland community has been incredibly generous! In 2022 we donated 40 boxes, in 2023 we donated 195 boxes, and in 2024 we donated at least 450 boxes plus additional car loads of food and toiletries. That's over 10.5 tonnes or $70,000 worth of food and essentials that have made it to local Maitland families in need.

How did this initiative start? ‚ñº

The Reverse Advent Calendar was started by Heather Luttrell, a school teacher from Ballarat, Victoria in 2018. She hoped to fill four boxes among family and friends but managed to fill 70! The idea spread across Australia, and last year over 6000 boxes were filled nationwide. Michelle Harvey, a school teacher and mum from East Maitland, started the Maitland drive and chose MRCS as the recipient organisation.

When do I need to register and when are boxes collected? ‚ñº

Registration details and collection dates will be provided closer to the drive period. We typically have multiple collection rounds to make it convenient for participants. You'll choose your preferred pickup location and collection round when you register.

Can businesses and community groups participate? ‚ñº

Absolutely! Many local businesses, schools, and community groups participate by registering for multiple boxes. You can register for as many boxes as you'd like to fill. The more the merrier - every box makes a real difference to a local family.
